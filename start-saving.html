Start Saving



The EPA's "Start Saving" page offers a detailed guide to water conservation, emphasizing how small changes can lead to significant savings in water, energy, and money. It outlines practical strategies for both indoor and outdoor water use, such as fixing leaks, installing WaterSense-labeled products, and adopting efficient irrigation practices.

The page highlights the dual benefits of water conservation: reducing water bills and saving energy, as treating and transporting water consumes substantial energy. Here’s a concise overview of the key points covered:

Indoor Water Conservation

Fixing Leaks: Leaks are a major source of water waste in homes. By repairing leaky faucets, showerheads, and toilets, homeowners can save thousands of gallons of water annually. For instance, a leaky faucet dripping at the rate of one drop per second can waste over 3,000 gallons of water a year.

Water-Efficient Appliances: Upgrading to WaterSense-labeled fixtures and appliances, such as showerheads, faucets, and toilets, can greatly reduce indoor water use. WaterSense products are certified to use at least 20% less water than standard models without compromising performance.

Behavioral Changes: Simple actions, like turning off the tap while brushing teeth, taking shorter showers, and only running dishwashers and washing machines with full loads, can also contribute to significant water savings.

Outdoor Water Conservation

Efficient Irrigation: Outdoor water use, especially for lawns and gardens, can account for up to 30% of a household’s water consumption. The EPA recommends using efficient irrigation systems, such as drip irrigation and soaker hoses, which deliver water directly to the roots, minimizing evaporation and runoff.

Smart Landscaping: Choosing native and drought-tolerant plants reduces the need for excessive watering. Additionally, applying mulch around plants helps retain soil moisture, reducing the frequency of watering.

Watering Practices: Watering plants in the early morning or late evening, when temperatures are cooler, reduces evaporation. The EPA also suggests using a rain gauge to ensure lawns are not overwatered, and installing a rain barrel to collect rainwater for irrigation.

Energy and Cost Savings

Energy Efficiency: Water conservation also impacts energy use. Heating water for showers, laundry, and dishwashing is energy-intensive. By using less hot water, homeowners can reduce their energy bills. Additionally, less energy is required to treat and transport water, benefiting the environment.

Cost Savings: By adopting water-efficient practices and products, households can reduce their water and energy bills. The EPA estimates that a family of four can save approximately 40,000 gallons of water and $380 annually by using WaterSense-labeled products and making small changes in water use.

Engaging in Fix a Leak Week

Water Conservation and the Environment: Reducing water use helps protect our water resources. In many parts of the country, water scarcity is a growing concern. By conserving water, we can help ensure that there is enough to meet the needs of both people and ecosystems.

Reducing Pollution: Efficient water use also reduces the amount of wastewater that needs to be treated, which can lower the amount of pollutants entering water bodies. This helps protect aquatic life and maintain healthy ecosystems.

Getting Started

Assess Your Water Use: The first step in conserving water is understanding how much you’re using. The EPA suggests conducting a water audit, which can help identify areas where water is being wasted.

Educate and Involve Others: Water conservation is a collective effort. Educating family members about the importance of saving water and involving them in conservation practices can amplify the impact. Schools, workplaces, and communities can also play a role by promoting water-saving initiatives.



By implementing these strategies, households can make a significant impact on water conservation, leading to savings in both water and energy costs while contributing to the sustainability of our water resources.
